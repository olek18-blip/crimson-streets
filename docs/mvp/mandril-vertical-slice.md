# Mandril Vertical Slice

This document translates the GDD MVP section into an actionable repository target.

## Canon Goal

Build a **Mandril-focused vertical slice** that proves:

- tone
- traversal
- driving
- police corruption fantasy
- faction collision
- one meaningful pursuit
- one strong narrative hook

This is the MVP that should be prioritized before any multi-city ambition.

---

## 1. Slice Boundaries

### Included
- one dense Mandril district
- one street market area
- one gang block
- one arterial road for vehicle movement
- one club/nightlife edge
- one police access point
- one short warehouse or back-lot combat space

### Excluded for MVP
- full country traversal
- port-scale industrial missions
- villa / elite infiltration spaces
- broad safehouse economy
- multiple major city transitions

---

## 2. Core Player Proof

The slice must prove these player actions:

- third-person movement
- sprint
- basic cover logic or cover-adjacent combat behavior
- weapon handling
- vehicle entry / exit
- urban driving
- objective following
- wanted-state understanding

If these are not solid, the rest of the concept does not matter yet.

---

## 3. Faction Proof

The slice must visibly establish:

- **Police** as the protagonist’s official environment
- **Street Gangs** as immediate visible city threat
- **Asuntos Internos** as future long-shadow pressure, even if only referenced

The player must feel they are not just fighting enemies, but operating between institutions and street power.

---

## 4. Mission Proof

The GDD points to **Dirty Patrol** and **Night Chase** as ideal MVP anchors.

### MVP Mission A — Dirty Patrol
Purpose:
- corruption onboarding
- police fantasy twisted from the inside
- first mission that teaches player what kind of game this is

Required beats:
- receive dirty order
- patrol under official cover
- collect protection money or pressure a target
- decide how forceful to be
- realize the system expects corruption as normal procedure

### MVP Mission B — Night Chase
Purpose:
- pursuit proof
- faction consequence
- narrative escalation

Required beats:
- identify fleeing target
- vehicle chase through Mandril
- pressure from police/gang response
- capture / kill / lose target consequence
- mission outcome reveals broader corruption stakes

---

## 5. Narrative Proof

The slice must communicate:

- Daniel Vega is already compromised
- corruption is institutional, not incidental
- Mandril is alive and dangerous
- the conspiracy goes higher than street crime
- the player wants to see the next layer

A vertical slice succeeds if the player ends wanting to know:
**Who is really pulling the strings above Daniel?**

---

## 6. Required Environment Palette for Mandril

According to the GDD and reference direction, Mandril should emphasize:

- dense neutral / amber urban layers
- wet asphalt
- night signage
- mixed civilian/combat spaces
- police access and public infrastructure

### Recommended scene mix
- one market street
- one public road with traffic pressure
- one nightlife edge
- one police-related entry zone
- one warehouse/back-lot confrontation space

---

## 7. UI Proof

The slice UI should minimally support:

- health
- ammo
- wanted state
- objective marker
- faction callouts
- mission prompts

The UI tone should feel like a premium crime thriller, not a placeholder arcade overlay.

---

## 8. Vertical Slice Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if a player can say:

1. I understand the tone of the game.
2. I understand who Daniel Vega is.
3. I understand why this is different from a generic crime game.
4. Moving, driving, and fighting in Mandril already feel promising.
5. I want to play the next mission.

---

## 9. Production Priority Order

1. Mandril district blockout
2. player controller and camera feel
3. driving feel
4. Dirty Patrol mission flow
5. Night Chase mission flow
6. faction readability in AI and UI
7. mission hook / reveal / final slice ending

---

## 10. Strategic Rule

Do not expand scope before the Mandril slice feels directed, tense, and ownable.

A polished 20-minute Mandril slice is more valuable than a broad but empty open world.
