# Character Style Guide

## Purpose

Define the playable and NPC visual language for **Crimson Streets** using a stylized noir low-poly approach.

The goal is to make every faction readable fast, memorable, and scalable for production.

---

## 1. Player Character

### Role
The player must feel like a dangerous operator caught between gangs and the police.

### Silhouette
- long coat or heavy jacket
- strong shoulder line
- narrow lower body
- visible sidearm or long weapon when equipped
- recognizable stance from behind

### Palette
- dark red / black base
- muted gold or warm accent
- minimal bright colors

### Key Identifiers
- coat hem and collar shape
- one dominant outerwear silhouette
- visible holster / weapon shape
- slightly more composed, centered stance than NPCs

### Animation Feel
- calm idle
- deliberate aim pose
- strong forward lean while running
- small coat sway

### Design Rule
The player should look like the visual midpoint between gang threat and police discipline.

---

## 2. Gang Faction

### Fantasy
Street predators, visible aggression, intimidation through posture and accessories.

### Silhouette Rules
- broad upper body
- layered jackets / hoodies / caps
- chain accessories or bold chest detail
- slightly wider stance than civilians
- some members can carry oversized props or weapons for profile variety

### Palette
- black
- red
- dirty orange
- metallic chain silver
- occasional muted yellow accent

### Archetypes
#### Gang Leader
- sunglasses or face-covering option
- chain accent
- strongest silhouette in the group
- cleaner, more intentional outfit than low-rank members

#### Second Boss / Enforcer
- suit or semi-formal criminal style
- cleaner profile
- colder presence
- should read as smarter / more dangerous rather than louder

#### Young Member
- hoodie / cap / slimmer build
- more casual, restless energy
- smaller frame for hierarchy contrast

#### Veteran Member
- thicker torso
- tougher stance
- less flashy, more brutal look

### Behavior Read
- chase stance should look aggressive
- attack posture should feel direct and confrontational
- group compositions should never look identical

---

## 3. Police Faction

### Fantasy
Tactical pressure, organized response, colder presence than gangs.

### Silhouette Rules
- armored vest / chest block
- helmet or police cap depending on unit
- cleaner vertical profile
- visible utility belt shape
- rifle-ready silhouette for advanced units

### Palette
- navy
- dark tactical gray
- cold blue highlights
- white police marking

### Archetypes
#### Tactical Leader
- heaviest armor presence
- most authoritative silhouette
- face shield / helmet option
- reads as high threat immediately

#### Standard Tactical Officer
- simpler silhouette than leader
- still clearly police from chest text and vest shape
- male and female variants should share faction identity first

#### K9 Unit (later)
- handler + dog silhouette
- used sparingly for high-pressure encounters

### Behavior Read
- patrol = stable and controlled
- chase = direct line and fast response
- attack = compact, disciplined firing posture

---

## 4. Civilians

### Fantasy
Everyday city life under pressure.

### Design Goal
They must contrast clearly with both gangs and police while still belonging to the same world.

### Silhouette Rules
- lighter body language
- softer clothing shapes
- less tactical geometry
- more neutral posture

### Palette
- muted green
- office blue
- gray-brown
- faded burgundy
- washed neutral tones

### Archetypes
#### Ally Female / Story Contact
- cleaner face readability
- practical clothing
- should stand out from generic crowd without looking like combat faction

#### Alley Woman
- rougher silhouette
- layered clothing
- more survival-driven posture

#### Office Man / Office Woman
- formal silhouettes for city contrast
- useful for downtown districts

#### Street Vendor
- prop-based silhouette from cart or stand
- helps district identity immediately

### Behavior Read
- flee animation should read clearly
- civilians should visually feel less planted than combat factions

---

## 5. Utility / Worker NPCs

### Fantasy
Working city background that makes the world feel larger and more believable.

### Silhouette Rules
- helmets
- safety vest blocks
- tools / clipboard / utility props
- readable from distance by hard hat color alone

### Palette
- yellow
- orange safety vest
- dusty blue workwear
- white foreman helmet

### Archetypes
- worker with helmet
- worker with vest
- foreman / supervisor

These characters are useful for:
- district flavor
- mission witnesses
- environmental storytelling
- future escort / protect / sabotage mission types

---

## 6. Proportion Rules

### General
Keep proportions stylized, not realistic.

Recommended direction:
- heads slightly larger than realism
- hands readable but not oversized cartoonishly
- torsos simplified into strong blocks
- legs slightly shortened for arcade readability

### Avoid
- noodle limbs
- tiny heads
- realistic anatomical complexity
- hyper-detailed fingers or faces for gameplay assets

---

## 7. Material Rules

### Recommended
- flat or lightly graded materials
- controlled edge highlights
- selective emissive accents
- simplified skin values

### Avoid
- realistic pores or noisy texture detail
- overuse of decals
- too many small accessories that vanish at game distance

---

## 8. Variant Strategy

To avoid clone syndrome, create production rules like this:

### Per faction
- 1 leader type
- 2 medium-tier variants
- 2 lower-tier variants
- 2 headwear options
- 2 color swaps
- 2 stance differences

This gives enough perceived diversity without a huge asset burden.

---

## 9. Mission-Critical Readability Rules

For any mission-critical enemy or ally:
- stronger accent color
- cleaner silhouette separation
- more visible head / collar / weapon profile
- better contrast against the environment

Mission-critical characters must be recognized faster than generic NPCs.

---

## 10. Production Priority

Build in this order:

1. Player base model
2. Police base model
3. Gang leader + one gang grunt
4. Civilian male + civilian female
5. Worker set
6. Variants and accessories

This order supports the core loop fastest.

---

## Final Rule
If a character only looks good in a close-up sheet but becomes unreadable during gameplay, redesign it.
